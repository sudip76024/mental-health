Surya Namaskar (The Sun Salutation)

A graceful sequence of twelve powerful yoga poses performed to energize the body and mind.

1. Pranamasana (Prayer Pose)

Stand at the edge of your mat, feet together, and bring your palms together in a prayer position at your chest.

2. Hasta Uttanasana (Raised Arms Pose)

Inhale and lift your arms up and back, keeping your biceps near your ears. Gently arch your back.

3. Hasta Padasana (Hand to Foot Pose)

Exhale and bend forward from your hips, bringing your hands to the floor beside your feet. Gently touch your forehead to your knees.

4. Ashwa Sanchalanasana (Equestrian Pose)

Inhale and step your right leg back, dropping the right knee to the ground. Look up, keeping your left knee bent at 90 degrees.

5. Dandasana (Stick Pose)

Exhale and bring your left leg back to meet the right, forming a straight line from head to heels (plank pose).

6. Ashtanga Namaskara (Salute with 8 parts)

Gently drop your knees, chest, and chin to the floor while exhaling. Your hips should be lifted slightly.

7. Bhujangasana (Cobra Pose)

Inhale, slide forward and up, lifting your chest while keeping your navel close to the floor. Shoulders away from ears.

8. Adho Mukha Svanasana (Downward-Facing Dog Pose)

Exhale and lift your hips up, forming an inverted 'V' shape with your body. Try to press your heels into the floor.

9. Ashwa Sanchalanasana (Equestrian Pose)

Inhale and step your right leg forward between your hands. Look up, keeping your left knee on the ground.

10. Hasta Padasana (Hand to Foot Pose)

Exhale and bring your left leg forward to meet your right. Touch your hands to the floor and your forehead to your knees.

11. Hasta Uttanasana (Raised Arms Pose)

Inhale, roll your spine up and lift your arms up and back, arching gently. Keep your biceps near your ears.

12. Tadasana (Mountain Pose)

Exhale and bring your arms down to your sides. Relax and stand tall. This concludes one cycle of the sequence.
